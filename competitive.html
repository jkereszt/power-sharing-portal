Competitive Landscape

Who else is in this space, where we can win, and what needs verification.

Last updated: June 21, 2026

⚠️ Working draft. Sections marked ✅ are based on confirmed knowledge. Sections marked ⚠️ require verification — the law only activated June 1, 2026 and the market is moving fast. Every team member is encouraged to bring URLs and intelligence to update this page.

The situation in one paragraph

No company has yet built specifically for § 42c EnWG Energy Sharing — the law is three weeks old. However, several well-resourced players already have the adjacent capabilities to move into this space quickly. The window for first-mover advantage is real but narrow.

German players to watch ✅⚠️

Lumenaza

Threat: High — verify immediately

A Berlin-based startup that already provides white-label billing and contract infrastructure for community solar ("Mieterstrommodell"). They have the billing layer, grid operator relationships, and regulatory knowledge. Most likely existing player to extend into § 42c.

Action: Check lumenaza.de — have they announced an Energy Sharing product? ⚠️

Enpal

Threat: High — distribution risk

Germany's largest solar-as-a-service provider with over 500,000 rooftop installations. They own the customer relationship with a massive base of solar prosumers. If Enpal adds Energy Sharing as a feature, they have instant distribution that no startup can match.

Action: Check enpal.de for any Energy Sharing announcements. ⚠️

1KOMMA5° (OnePointFiveDegrees)

Threat: High

A fast-growing German startup integrating solar, battery, heat pump, and EV charging into one smart home energy platform. Well-funded, platform-first approach. Energy Sharing is a natural extension of their ecosystem play.

Action: Check 1komma5grad.com for any Energy Sharing product. ⚠️

Sonnen (owned by Shell) ✅

Threat: Medium

Home battery storage + virtual community. Their "SonnenCommunity" lets battery owners share stored energy across a virtual pool — national scale rather than local neighbor-to-neighbor. Different architecture but competing for the same solar owner's attention.

Next Kraftwerke (owned by RWE) ✅

Threat: Medium–High

Virtual power plant aggregator with sophisticated energy data infrastructure, now part of RWE. Could be deployed for neighborhood Energy Sharing at scale with RWE's backing.

Incumbent energy suppliers ⚠️

All major German utilities are aware of § 42c EnWG. None have confirmed products yet, but the threat is real.

The Austrian model — 5 years ahead ✅

Austria introduced Energiegemeinschaften in 2021 — the same concept, five years earlier. This is our most important reference market for what Germany will look like in 2027–2028.

Austrian platforms to research: Enpuls (subsidiary of Linz AG) and Smartmixer (startup for energy community management). ⚠️

Key question: Are any Austrian platforms planning to expand into Germany? They have a head start of 5 years.

The unoccupied high ground ⚠️

The most strategically important question: who is building the virtual balancing area infrastructure?

The virtual balancing area model would fix most of § 42c's current limitations and is actively supported by citizen energy groups. No confirmed platform has positioned itself as the infrastructure provider for this model. This appears to be open territory.
