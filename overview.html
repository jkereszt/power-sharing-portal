What we are building

A platform that enables solar panel owners to sell their excess electricity directly to neighbors — simply, legally, and at fair prices for both sides.

Germany's new Energy Sharing law (§ 42c EnWG) came into force on June 1, 2026. For the first time, this is legally possible without going through a traditional energy supplier. We are building the platform that makes it easy.

The opportunity

Over 5 million solar installations exist in Germany today. Owners who generate more electricity than they use currently feed it back to the grid and receive less than 8 cents per kilowatt-hour — below their production cost. Meanwhile, their neighbors pay around 30 cents per kilowatt-hour to their energy supplier.

Energy Sharing closes this gap: the solar owner earns more, the neighbor pays less, and we run the platform that makes it happen.
